OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY POLICY

At LIFEX, we recognize that our commitment to the safety and well-being of our employees is paramount to our success and sustainability. As a company engaged in the production and sale of composite strapping, we are dedicated to maintaining an environment that prioritizes occupational safety and health for all our employees, contractors, and visitors.

1. Policy Statement

Our Occupational Safety Policy is designed to comply with all applicable local, state, and federal safety regulations and industry standards. It is our responsibility to minimize workplace hazards, provide effective training, and ensure that safety measures are continuously monitored and improved.

2. Goals

    Our principal goals include:
  • To prevent workplace injuries and illnesses through proactive safety practices.
  • To establish a culture of safety that encourages all employees to actively participate in and contribute to safety measures.
  • To conduct regular audits and risk assessments, ensuring that all equipment and processes meet or exceed safety standards.

3. Responsibilities

  • Management: It is the responsibility of management to lead by example, providing the resources needed for a safe working environment and ensuring compliance with safety policies.
  • Employees: All employees are expected to comply with safety policies, report unsafe conditions, and participate in training programs.
  • Safety Officer: Our designated Safety Officer will oversee the implementation of safety protocols, conduct safety training, and serve as a point of contact for safety concerns.

4. Training and Education

    We are committed to providing comprehensive training for all employees, which includes:
  • Safe handling and storage of composite strapping materials.
  • Proper use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
  • Emergency response procedures and first aid training.
  • Regular safety drills and refreshers on safety protocols.

5. Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

We will conduct routine inspections and risk assessments to identify potential hazards associated with the production and handling of composite strapping. Our findings will guide our safety improvements and actions.

6. Incident Reporting and Investigation

All incidents, accidents, or near misses must be reported immediately. Comprehensive investigations will be conducted to determine the root cause and to implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

7. Continuous Improvement

We are committed to continuously improving our occupational safety policies and practices. Regular reviews, employee feedback, and adaptation to new regulations will ensure that our policies remain effective.
